# Brief

## Foreground
Show the work, and communicate a tasteful vibe. The site should feel
like a designer who thinks with their hands — the vignettes are the
proof. The most concrete weak spot today is typographic discipline:
the type on the current site feels under-considered compared to the
references, and tightening it is the highest-leverage move.

## Supporting cast & register
Quiet, warm, tasteful — with small moments of personality that reward
attention. Emil's restraint is the baseline; borrow a little of
Daniel's willingness to show character; inherit Brian's typographic
craft (minus the dark mode).

## References (positive)
- https://emilkowal.ski — the anchor. Cream bg, muted serif-adjacent
  type, ruthless whitespace, diary-like intimacy. Current site is
  already closest to this and should stay in this family.
- https://www.danield.design — pulled for the willingness to show
  personality and warmth (color tiles, layout confidence). Not the
  maximalism — the courage to not be uniformly flat.
- https://brianlovin.com — pulled purely for typographic craft and
  attention to detail. NOT for dark mode or the dashboard framing.

## References (anti)
- Anything dark-mode by default
- Loud gradients or splashy heroes (Daniel approaches the line; don't cross it)
- Corporate / templated / "dribbble SaaS" energy
- Type that fights itself — too many weights, too many sizes,
  unclear hierarchy

## Criteria
- Compared to Emil, does the typography show the same disciplined
  hierarchy and rhythm — tight scale, consistent weight logic,
  clear foreground/supporting — or is type still fighting itself?
- Compared to Daniel, does the page reward scrolling with 1–2 small
  moments of warmth or personality, or does it read as uniformly
  flat and safe?
- Does the hero + opening moment establish "designing with machines"
  clearly, or could this be any designer's portfolio?
- Does each vignette feel like a considered artifact, or like a
  screenshot pasted into a card?

## Dealbreakers
- Dark mode by default
- Loud gradients, splashy heroes, or any "look at me" visual shouting
- Feels corporate / templated / generic SaaS
- Undisciplined typography: mixed sizes fighting, no clear rhythm,
  too many weights

## Mode
Exploration → convergence. User knows the direction (Emil-anchored,
with personality and craft) but the specific execution needs to be
discovered by iterating.
